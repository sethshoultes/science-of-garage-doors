**Ever wonder what's really happening when you press that button on your garage door remote?** There's a lot more going on than meets the eye — from torsion spring physics to electromagnetic interference. We built an interactive 3D experience to show you exactly how it all works.

## What You'll Learn

### How Your Door Actually Moves

Our interactive door system model lets you open and close a sectional garage door and watch what happens in real time. You'll see how the five panels hinge along the track, and live readouts show you door position, panel angle, cable tension, and spring torque as the door moves. It's the kind of thing most people never think about — until something breaks.

### The Physics of Torsion Springs

Torsion springs are the unsung heroes of your garage door. They store and release energy to lift hundreds of pounds of door weight, and they do it tens of thousands of times over their lifespan.

Our spring physics simulator lets you adjust the door weight from 80 to 400 lbs and watch how the spring responds. You'll see how turns, stored energy, torque, and wire stress all change — and why spring sizing matters so much for safety and longevity.

### Chain vs. Belt vs. Screw vs. Direct Drive

Not all garage door openers work the same way. Our side-by-side 3D models show you the mechanical differences between the four main drive types:

- **Chain Drive** — The classic workhorse with sprocket and chain
- **Belt Drive** — Quieter operation with a reinforced rubber belt
- **Screw Drive** — A threaded steel rod with fewer moving parts
- **Direct Drive** — The motor itself moves along the rail

Seeing them in motion makes it immediately clear why some are quieter, some are more durable, and some need more maintenance.

### Inside the Motor

We built an exploded-view model of a PSC (Permanent Split Capacitor) motor — the type found in most residential openers. Toggle between assembled and exploded views to see the stator coils, squirrel cage rotor, worm gear reduction, capacitor, circuit board, and limit switches. Run the motor to watch the rotor spin and the gears turn.

### Smart Home Integration

Modern garage door openers connect to Wi-Fi, HomeKit, and cloud services. Our 3D visualization maps out the full IoT ecosystem — hub, phone, router, remote, and safety sensors — so you can see how all the pieces talk to each other.

### Why Your LED Bulbs Might Be Killing Your Remote Range

This one surprises most people. Certain LED bulbs emit radio frequency interference that can cut your garage door remote's range from 50 feet down to 3 feet. Our interactive demo lets you swap between different bulb wattages and watch the signal strength drop in real time. If your remote has been acting up since you switched to LEDs, this is probably why.

### Interactive Troubleshooting Guide

We also included a built-in troubleshooting guide to help you diagnose common garage door problems on your own. Just click the symptom you're experiencing and follow the diagnostic path:

- **Door won't open or close** — Is the motor running but the door not moving? No response at all? Motor humming but not turning? Each scenario points to a different cause, from a disconnected trolley to a blown capacitor.
- **Door reverses immediately** — Usually a safety sensor or force setting issue. The guide walks you through checking sensor alignment, obstructions, and limit adjustments.
- **Remote control not working** — Before you replace the batteries, the guide helps you rule out LED interference, range issues, and reprogramming needs.
- **Door is noisy or grinding** — Grinding, squealing, and rattling all mean different things. The guide helps you pinpoint whether it's the rollers, hinges, chain, or spring.
- **myQ / Wi-Fi keeps disconnecting** — Smart opener losing connection? The guide covers router placement, signal strength, and firmware updates.

It's like having a garage door tech walk you through the basics before you pick up the phone.
